    About the race

    About the race #

    Cold Hawaii Ultra is a unique trail running race taking you either from

    • Bulbjerg via Vester Thorup to Agger (100 km),
    • Bulbjerg to Agger (50 miles), 5
    • Hanstholm to Agger (50 km), 5
    • Nr. Vorupør to Agger (24 km) 5 eller
    • Lyngby to Agger (12 km)

    by the old lifeboat road and the West Coast Trail on the northwest coast of Denmark.

    The race is a low-key trail running race without all the fuss. It is all about the incredible nature and the trails in Thy National Park. The course is clearly marked with small flags and 5 aid stations are offered. All Cold Hawaii Ultra finishers receive a small trophy at the end.

    Be aware that the first 20 km will not be marked for runners doing 100 km. Instead, participants will have to navigate this part themselves by using a GPS unit or a GPS running watch. The last 80 km will be clearly marked. A GPS unit is not necessary if you are doing either 50 miles, 50 km, 24 km, or 12 km.

    The course is described in detail on the Course page.

    Race bibs and timing chip #

    If you indicate that you want to take the bus to the start area, you will receive your race bib and timing chip when getting on the bus. If you do not sign up for the bus, you can pick up your race bib and timing chip from the race organizers in the starting area.

    If you decide last minute to not take the bus, even though you did sign up, no worries! Your race bib will still make it to the starting area, together with the bus.

    Aid stations #

    At the aid stations you can find

    • Bananas
    • Coke
    • Water (bring your own cup or bottle - no single-use cups are offered!)
    • Energy drink
    • Wine gums
    • Potato chips
    • Mini müsli bars from Nordthy

    For runners doing 50 miles or 100 km it is also possible to get 1 dropbag delivered to the aid station in Klitmøller.

    Dropbags have to be safely packaged and clearly marked with your bib number and name. The maximum capacity is 30 L. Dropbags are handed over to the race organizers in the starting area and will be brought to the finishing area as soon as possible after its use.

    Equipment and safety #

    The aid stations are far apart and at times so is civilization. For this reason, it is mandatory for participants doing 50 km, 50 miles, or 100 km to bring:

    • 1 wind-proof jacket
    • 1 buff or beanie
    • Bottles or a drinking system with a capacity of minimum 1.5 L. No single-use cups are offered at the aid stations.
    • Extra energy and food
    • 1 cellphone
    • A first aid kit: at a minimum, a compress dressing and sports tape
    • 50 miles and 100 km: headlamp
    • 100 km: GPS unit or watch

    For participants doing 24 km it is mandatory bring a bottle or drinking system with a capacity of minimum 0.5 L. No single-use cups are offered at the aid station. For participants doing 12 km no mandatory gear requirements are in place. However, we strongly advice dressing according to the weather—the race area is remote and vehicle access is limited, so you may have to wait a while for help to reach you.

    A headlamp is necessary for participants doing 50 miles or 100 km. The sun rises at around 07:00 and sets around 19:30.

    It is strictly necessary to use a GPS unit for participants doing 100 km as the first 20 km will not be marked. Both dedicated GPS units or GPS watches are permitted. A unit without a background map is sufficient, but we recommend bringing a backup unit with a background map, e.g., a cellphone.

    For this purpose, we recommend the app Topo GPS which is available for both Android and iOS. The app allows you to import a GPX file and offers offline topographic maps of Denmark for DKK 29.

    GPX files are found on the Course page.
